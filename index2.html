Hand stitching types

1.Basting stitch: it's used to temporarily hold together pieces that may shift as you use your sewing machine; it's recommended to use a contrasting thread so it's easier to remove.

2.Running stitch: it's done in the same way as the basting stitch, but with smaller stitches and with a locking stitch at the end.

3.Backstitch: it creates a ver strong seam and it's used on heavy fabrics.

4.Overcast stitch: it's used to finish cut edges that tend to ravel.

5.Ladder stitch: it's used to create an "invisible" seam between two edges.

6.Blind stitch: it also creates "invisible" stitches.

7.Securing stitch: regardless of the stitch you choose to use, you should alwas end it with a securing stitch, so that it prevents your work from coming undone.
